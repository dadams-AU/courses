POSC 315: Introduction to Public Policy Handouts

Week 1-1 Introduction Day

We introduce the course, go over the syllabus and contact information. Basic first day of class.

Slide Deck

Week 1-2 Overview of Public Policy

We get right into the materials covering values of the American political regime and the content of Birkland chapter 1.

Slide Deck

Week 2-1 Policy Process Elements

This lecture brings us to the idea of the policy process, what it is and how we conceptualize it. This presentation covers Birkland chapter 2.

Slide Deck

Week 2-2 Policy Process Theories

This lecture focuses on theories of the policy process, including incrementalism, Kingdon's Three Streams Model, Jones and Baumgartner's Punctuated Equilibrium, and Sabatier's Advocacy Coalition Framework. This presentation covers some of Birkland chapter 11 as well as additional readings

Slide Deck

Week 3-1 Historical and Structural Elements

Here we look at the institutional and structural elements than enable and enjoin policy making in the United States. This presentation covers Birkland Chapter 3.

Slide Deck

Week 3-2 Official Actors in the Policy Process

The focus is on Chapter 4 of Birkland's text. We cover theories of interaction among actors and the offical actors: legislators, executives, bureaucrats, and judges.

Slide Deck

Week 4-1 Unofficial Actors in the Policy Process

The focus is on Chapter 5 of Birkland's text. We cover interaction concepts and the unofficial actors in the policy process, including the news media, interest groups, think tanks, etc.

Slide Deck

Week 4-2 Role of Individuals and MLK's Letter from Birmingham Jail

This lecture focuses on the role of individuals in the policy process, with a special emphasis on Martin Luther King Jr.'s "Letter from Birmingham Jail" as a case study.

Slide Deck

Week 6-1 Agenda Setting

The focus is on Chapter 6 of Birkland's text. We get into theories of the group, problems, agenda and agenda setting, conflict and competition, political power, and preview some change theories.

Slide Deck

Week 6-2 Policy Typologies

Depending on the problem definition and the ability to reach the agenda, policies forged in the policy process emerge as a type. Here we explore these types, discuss their nature, and compare them with an eye on the predicting political behavior.

Slide Deck

Week 7-1 Decision-Making I

This week we cover decision-making. The focus is on rational decision-making theories. We also get into two types of dysfunctional decision-making ideas.

Slide Deck

Week 7-2 Decision-Making II

This week we cover decision-making. The focus is on rational decision-making theories. We also get into two types of dysfunctional decision-making ideas.

Slide Deck

Week 9-1 Policy Analysis I

This week we take decision making a step further by exploring policy analysis and looking through the lens of a policy analyst.

Slide Deck

Week 9-2 Policy Analysis II

This week we take decision making a step further by exploring policy analysis and looking through the lens of a policy analyst.

Slide Deck

Week 11-1 Policy Design & Tools

Here we look at the nature policy design, the tradeoffs that go with it, and how it leads to the questions we ask about the tools used to achive a policy objective

Slide Deck

Week 11-2 Implementation

Implementation is the process of putting policy into action. Here we look at the complex nature of implementation, the tradeoffs that go with it, and how it leads to the questions we ask about the tools used to achive a policy objective.

Slide Deck

Week 12-1 Failure and Learning

Can we every really have a _good_ policy? Why do policies fail? How can we learn from our mistakes? These are the questions explored in this lecture.

Slide Deck

Week 12-2 Evaluation and Science

What is policy evaluation? What is the nature of knowing. This week we explore deep questions about public policy and science. We ask, "Can we ever really know if we have good policy?"
